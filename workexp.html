Dignity Health, Arizona, USA

Research Assistant (Data Engineering): June 2024 –June 2026

Behavioral studies have consistently shown that whereas we often remember the gist of emotional stimuli, we forget the details. This is exemplified by the “weapon focus” effect, in which crime eye-witnesses remember the central emotional content of the experience (weapon used), but not the peripheral details (perpetrator’s clothing). While research on this phenomenon has focused on well-known brain regions like the amygdala and hippocampus, my project analysed brain signals from the pulvinar, a lesser-studied region known to be involved in visual and emotional processing.
