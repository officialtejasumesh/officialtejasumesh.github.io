About Me

Hello! I’m an incoming Ph.D. student in the Computer Science department at the University of Melbourne. My research focuses on the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Neuroscience, with a specific interest in understanding the interaction between brain regions during emotional stimuli. Broadly, I'm interested in:

  1. AI & Neuroscience
  2. Robotics and Embodied AI (Multi-agent environments)
  3. Neural Signal Processing

Before heading to Melbourne, I worked as a Research Assistant in Dr. Andrew Yang’s lab at Barrow. My work there involved neural signal processing in MATLAB, specifically computing Phase Locking Values (PLV) and analyzing synchronization between the amygdala and pulvinar.

I am actively engaged in developing research for automated fact-checking and episodic memory in embodied AI, with a long-term goal of bridging academic research and professional AI industry applications.
